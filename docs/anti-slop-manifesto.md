# Sonar Mag anti-slop manifesto

Read this before writing any Sonar Mag article. Prefer silence over filler.

## What we are

An independent desk. Essays and notes with a point of view, not SEO paste or chatbot wallpaper. Readers should feel a human chose the angle, the evidence, and the ending.

## Hard bans

- No "In today's fast-paced world," "In an age of…," "It's important to note," "At the end of the day."
- No fake authority: "experts say," "many believe," "studies show" without a named source.
- No balanced-to-death both-sides that refuse a claim. Take a position or ask a real question.
- No synonym salads, emoji-as-emphasis, or listicles that could be a bullet dump without prose.
- No copying source essays. Frame, quote/embed, then argue in Sonar’s voice.
- No inventing quotes, stats, or titles. If you cannot verify it, cut it.
- No engagement bait titles ("You won't believe…"). Wit is fine; clickfraud is not.

## Positive craft

- **One job per piece.** A reader should finish knowing what changed in their understanding.
- **Concrete first.** Names, numbers, dates, places before abstractions.
- **Short sentences earn long ones.** Vary rhythm; kill throat-clearing openers.
- **Embeds are evidence, not decoration.** A tweet belongs where it advances the argument.
- **End clean.** Last paragraph lands the claim — no "only time will tell."

## Voice

Editorial, dry when useful, never sneering at the reader. Serif on the page; spine in the copy. Prefer "the data show X" over "we need to have a conversation about X."

## Length

Usually 5–9 prose paragraphs (plus embeds). If you need 20, you have two pieces.

## Checklist (pass all)

1. Could this exist only because a model was asked to "write an article"? If yes, rewrite.
2. Is every stat or quote attributable?
3. Does the title earn the body (and vice versa)?
4. Would you publish this under your own name?
